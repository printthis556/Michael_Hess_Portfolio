About Me

Hi, I’m Michael Hess. I sit at the intersection of IT, operations, and software—where small improvements add up to big wins. By day, I manage dealership systems and vendors (vAuto, 1Micro, VinPoint, Reynolds), own local IT needs, and keep projects moving. I’ve helped cut vehicle check-in time by ~40% and negotiated vendor contracts to lower service costs by ~15%.

I’m also a software engineering student focused on C/C++, Python, and front-end fundamentals (HTML/CSS/JS). What excites me most is the creative side of programming—taking an idea from scratch and shaping it into something tangible. I enjoy designing clean, intuitive UIs, experimenting with ways to simplify workflows, and building tools that don’t just work, but feel satisfying to use. For me, coding is both problem-solving and creative expression, and I’m driven by turning that mix into solutions that make teams faster and outcomes measurable.

On the creative side, I run a TikTok channel as a side gig for fun. I used to do TikTok content creation full-time, which sharpened my storytelling, lighting, editing, and analytics chops. These days my content revolves around 3D prints, AutoCAD builds, and eye-catching advertisements. Along the way, I’ve built international relationships and business partnerships that let me collaborate beyond borders. It’s a creative outlet that keeps my communication sharp, lets me experiment with new tools, and connects me with a global community that values practical, no-fluff content.

Outside of work, I’m a licensed pilot—aviation taught me to prioritize safety, clarity, and calm problem-solving under pressure. Flying also sharpened my multitasking skills, balancing navigation, communication, and split-second decision-making in dynamic environments. I’m exploring remote-friendly roles where I can combine technical skills, operations experience, and a service-first mindset to deliver results.

Projects

I’m showcasing a selection of my successful videos and products that I’ve designed and created on TikTok. These highlight my work in areas like 3D printing, AutoCAD design, and creative content production.

More projects will be added soon—stay tuned!

Xbox Controller Gimbal Joystick (5M+ Views)This is my most popular TikTok video, with over 5 million views. At the time, I was really into Microsoft Flight Simulator and wanted to make the experience feel more realistic. I discovered a gimbal joystick design for Xbox controllers, 3D printed it, and created a video showcasing the build. It remains one of my favorite 3D prints to this day.
Custom Halloween Light-Up DecorationWhile browsing stores, I saw small light-up Halloween decorations and decided I could create my own. I sketched the initial design in Procreate on my iPad, then refined and finalized the cut paths in LightBurn. Using my laser cutter, I brought the design to life and added two RGB LED pods on the back for customizable colors. This project remains one of my favorite builds and a great example of combining digital design with hands-on fabrication.
Shaving Station (30K+ Views)This project started after I created an advertisement for a head shaver but realized I didn’t have a proper stand or way to rinse it after use. Using Fusion 360, I designed a custom shaving station that solved the problem. The stand holds the head shaver securely with a cup below for easy rinsing, a side holder to dry a razor, and a small bowl above for storing spare single-blade razors. The design not only performed well in the video (over 30,000 views) but also became a practical product I still use every day.
Ekster Wallets AdvertisementThis was one of my most successful advertisements. After discovering Ekster wallets online and being impressed by their design, I reached out to the company to explore a partnership. We collaborated on an advertisement to promote their wallets, which turned out to be a great success. I still use their wallets today and continue to admire their quality and durability.
Multi-Size Covered Can HolderThis project came from solving a real problem I experienced: while enjoying a canned drink outdoors, a bug flew inside without me noticing. After that, I wanted a practical way to keep cans covered and protected. I designed a holder that accommodates multiple can sizes—tall, skinny, and standard—while keeping them sealed so bugs can’t get in. I also added a removable mount so it can be secured when needed. It’s become an everyday essential for me, and one of the most useful products I’ve designed.
